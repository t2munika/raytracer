CS 488: Extended Raytracer project

Created by Tara Munikar

Winter 2014

Objectives

  1. Extra primitives: Cones and Cylinders


  2. Texture mapping


  3. Bump Mapping
    • There are two examples of bump mapping shown here.
    • The one on the right uses a grey scale height map shown below and is calculated using Blinn's method of finding partial derivatives to determine preturbation of the normal
    • The one of the left uses the more commonly used normal map, which explicitly defines the normals using RGB values



  4. Constructive Solid Geometry (from top to bottom: intersection, difference, union)


  5. Depth of Field (left: no Depth of Field applied, right: Depth of Field applied at the center sphere, with a aperture size of 0.3)


  6. Glossy Reflection (left: mirror reflection, right: Glossy Reflection using 8 reflected rays)


  7. Anti-Aliasing using Supersampling and Jitter (left: no anti-aliasing, right: 16 sample rays)


  8. Refraction

  9. Final Scene (left: without Depth of Field, right: with Depth of Field)
